State Restrictions

Facing a fourth straight year of drought, the State Water Board adopted water-use reduction mandates on May 5 to implement Gov. Jerry Brown's April 1 executive order for a 25 percent statewide reduction in urban water use. In the San Diego region, the state's mandate translates to water-saving targets between 12 and 36 percent (compared to 2013 levels) for the San Diego County Water Authority's retail member agencies starting June 1 and lasting through February 2016. These targets will be challenging to meet, but everyone's efforts to save more water add up to make a huge difference.

 

At its special Board meeting in May, the Water Authority's Board of Directors adopted a set of measures to help local water agencies meet the state mandated water-use reduction targets. These included restricting irrigation of landscapes with potable water to no more than two days a week across the region, and boosting its conservation and outreach efforts.

Local water agencies are responsible for determining the specific measures necessary to meet state mandates and avoid financial penalties by the State Water Resources Control Board.
